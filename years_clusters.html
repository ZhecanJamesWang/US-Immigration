Important Years and Clustering

Our second project story

Posted by Zhecan Wang, Nur Shalapobersky, Filippos Lymperopoulos on April 24, 2016

Overview

Previously in our project, we all went to explore different datasets like bills, newspaper articles and so on and did some simple analyzing.

Now that we had all of our data pre-processed and ready to analyze we wanted to begin with a basic exploration over time in order to point out years and trends that we could explore further.

We had decided that the best way to complement the other immigration team’s work was to find the years which have been most influential in shaping and changing the United States’ stance on immigration and foreigners.

We each separately constructed scripts to find important years to analyzed our separate datasets and found correlations.

We also start using clustering and visualization with d3.

Next steps, in order to better find relationships among years, we choose to do document clustering. We used tf-idf to generate document feature vector matrix for clustering purpose.

We choose K-means to experiment first. After tuning the parameters, the result of clustering articles from 1997 - 2002 are followings:

The five clusters are all very interesting and the words really make themselves into consistent groups. These are very exciting! I also plot the year distribution of these clusters. As followings:

As you can see, some of the clusters center around specific years and some others are a bit general. The more specific ones seem definitely more interesting.
