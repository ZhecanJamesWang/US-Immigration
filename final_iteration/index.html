A Shift of Opinions: Immigration and the United States
This election cycle has once again cast a limelight upon the issue of immigration in the United States, referencing both legal and undocumented immigrants, and even refugees fleeing war-torn countries. It’s amazing that we take the rhetoric that some politicians display as the norm nowadays, since 30 years ago, this debate was aired.
Immigration Fluctuation
It's not only rhetoric that has varied greatly over the years. Immigration rates themselves have fluctuated greatly in recent years, varying by over 150 million in the past thirty years alone.

When did the United States' attitude towards immigrants become so polarized, and sometimes hostile? It's necessary to investigate this question through multiple routes.
